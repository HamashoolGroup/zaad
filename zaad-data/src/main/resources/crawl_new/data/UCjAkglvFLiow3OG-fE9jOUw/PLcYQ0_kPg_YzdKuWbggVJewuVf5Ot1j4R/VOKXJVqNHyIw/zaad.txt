--- VIDEO TITLE: English Lessons with inlingua Vancouver - Two Of The Most Common Grammar Patterns in English: "In terms of_noun/v.+ING_; When it comes to _v.+ING_ Teacher: Troy --- VIDEO DESCRIPTION: This video teaches how to use "in terms of" and "when it comes to" effectively. If you really start paying attention to the English that is all around you, like on T.V., commercials, TED videos, newspaper articles, online articles, spoken English, or virtually any other source of authentic, native-level English, you will hear these two grammar/idiom patterns absolutely everywhere. They are simply the #1 "go-to" patterns for native English speakers all around the world. Obviously, then, it behooves any student of real, spoken (and written) English to learn these thoroughly in three ways: "Say it, write it, and catch it in real life." The nice thing is, due to their commonality and usefulness, any student can create a huge jump in their skills just by learning and using these two grammar patterns effectively in both speaking and writing. Also discussed are some additional points, like "When it CAME to" (simple past0, and "cousin patterns", like As far as_____goes,_____, and As far as _____ is concerned, __________. Have fun adding these awesome structures to your life, and see what happens to the effectiveness of your English communication. Connect with us!
