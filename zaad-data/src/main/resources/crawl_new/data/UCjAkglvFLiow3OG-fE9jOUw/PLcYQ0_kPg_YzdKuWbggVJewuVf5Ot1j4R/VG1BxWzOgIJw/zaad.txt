--- VIDEO TITLE: English Writing Skills: Commas (Part 1 of 2) - English lessons with inlingua Vancouver --- VIDEO DESCRIPTION: English Writing Skills: Commas (Part 1 of 2) - English lessons with inlingua Vancouver Use: use a comma before a coordinating conjuction that introduces an independent clause (that's a clause that can stand alone as a complete sentence). And, but, for, or, so, yet. My sister's name is Miranda, and my brother's name is Austin. I rushed home, and I finished my homework before soccer practice. I am very good in Spanish, but I'm not good in French. Kate slept late, but Meg got up early. We went to the beach last summer. I finally learned to windsurf. Use after conjunctive adverbs (adverbs working as conjuctions-linking two sentences. However, finally, furthermore, indeed, meanwhile, nevertheless, thus). I thought I did well in the test; However, I got a C. After introductory phrases Since my mother forgot to pack me a dessert, I ate your Twinkie. --- TEACHER: TASH Connect with us!
