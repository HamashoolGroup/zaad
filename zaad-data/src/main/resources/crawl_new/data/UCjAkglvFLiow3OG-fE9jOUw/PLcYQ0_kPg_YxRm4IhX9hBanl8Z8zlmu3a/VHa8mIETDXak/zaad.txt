Everyday, every day, and all day
Published on Sep 3, 2015
--- VIDEO TITLE: Everyday, Every day, and All day - English Lessons with inlingua Vancouver --- VIDEO DESCRIPTION: Every day / All day / Everyday All day is a phrase that usually acts as an adverb. It means the entire day, or a large part of the day Every day means each day, on all of the days over a certain period of time weeks, months, years) Example: I study English every day. He goes to the gym every day. Everyday is an adjective; therefore, it must go before a noun it is describing. It means ordinary, normal or a common place. It refers to something used or done routinely. Example: It is not an everyday event. A wedding dress is not a everyday dress. * Pronunciation difference between Everyday and Every day is on the placement of the stress. Everyday: the stress is on the first syllable EVeryday Every day: the stress is on the word day every DAY Connect with us!
