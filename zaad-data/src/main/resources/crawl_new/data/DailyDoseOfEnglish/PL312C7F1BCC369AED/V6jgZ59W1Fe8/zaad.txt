An irregular verb is a verb that does not add -ed to the root verb to form the past simple and past participle. You have to learn each irregular verb individually. The infinitive form is to drive. The past simple form is drove. The past participle form is driven. Drive, drove, driven. I drive a red car. I am driving a car. I drove my car. I was driving my car yesterday. I have driven a car. I had driven a car. I will drive a car. I am going to drive a car. This time next week, I will have driven a car. Drive the car! Can you drive? Did you drive your car yesterday?
