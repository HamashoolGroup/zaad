Learn More: http://linguaspectrum.com British English Pronunciation Course: http://soundsbritish.com Index of Daily Doses: http://DailyDoseOfEnglish.com This Daily Dose of English answers a video request from Dato in Georgia. He wanted to know how we use "for" and "to" with "me". It's much more complicated than it first seems. "For" and "to" are used in so many ways, that I thought it would be better if I presented some examples of their usage. You will then be able to see how they are used. I can't open this. Can you do it for me? | Okay. Give it to me. I'll try. This letter needs posting. Can you do it for me? | Post the letter? Yes, give it to me. She cooks dinner for me every day. | It looks to me like you've found the right woman. Check this essay for me, would you? | Okay. Well, this doesn't sound right to me. You can't have any cake. It's all for me. | It doesn't matter to me. I don't like cake. The police are coming. They want to arrest me. They're coming for me. | It sounds like you deserve it to me. It's too early for me to talk about marriage. | But I thought you wanted to be married to me. This is the place for me. | It doesn't look very nice to me. Play the piano for me. | And sing to me, too. I'll take half for me... | and give half to you.
