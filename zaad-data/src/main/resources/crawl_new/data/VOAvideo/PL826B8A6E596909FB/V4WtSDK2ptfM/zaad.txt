Obama, Rouhani Face Domestic Opposition to Nuclear Deal
Published on Nov 6, 2014
U.S. President Barack Obama and Iranian President Hassan Rouhani both face domestic conservative opposition to a potential deal limiting Iran's nuclear program in exchange for easing some economic sanctions. Now that Republicans are set to take over control of the U.S. Senate - putting them in control of both houses of Congress - VOA State Department Correspondent Scott Stearns reports on how the change might affect those talks.
