Divided Lebanese Neighborhood Finds Peace in Kitchen
Published on Jan 1, 2015
Long-running clashes between two neighborhoods in Lebanon's second largest city, Tripoli, have been further fueled by the war in Syria. But inside a kitchen on a Tripoli street that is the front line of the conflict, women from both communities are working together to earn a living. John Owens has more for VOA from Tripoli.
