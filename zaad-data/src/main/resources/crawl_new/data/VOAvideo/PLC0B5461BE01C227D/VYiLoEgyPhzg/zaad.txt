Aceh Rebuilt Decade After Tsunami, But Scars Remain
Published on Dec 17, 2014
On December 26, 2004 there was an earthquake in the Indian Ocean so powerful it caused the Earth?s axis to wobble a few centimeters. Onshore on the island of Sumatra, the resulting tsunami was devastating. A decade later, VOA Correspondent Steve Herman reports from Banda Aceh, Indonesia, where although there is little remaining evidence of the physical devastation, the psychological scars among survivors remain.
