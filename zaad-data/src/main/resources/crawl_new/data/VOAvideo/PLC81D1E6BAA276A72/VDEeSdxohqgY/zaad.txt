Pakistan Flood Aftermath Creates Danger of Epidemics
Uploaded on Sep 13, 2010
Waterborne illnesses are making the flooding victims in Pakistan increasingly desperate for medical help. Health and social workers warn that if aid does not come soon, the outbreak of potentially fatal diseases is imminent. Prevention costs very little. But if these diseases do break out, cramped conditions make it possible for them to spread very quickly. VOA's Vidushi Sinha has more.
