Antipsychotic Drugs Prescribed For Children Linked to Weight Gain
Uploaded on Jul 8, 2010
Researchers have found that some antipsychotic drugs frequently prescribed for children and teenagers are causing them to gain a disturbing amount of weight. And that puts them at risk for diabetes and heart disease later in life. While doctors have known for some time that these types of medications cause weight gain in adults, there has been little research until now on how they affect kids and teens. VOA's Melinda Smith has details.
