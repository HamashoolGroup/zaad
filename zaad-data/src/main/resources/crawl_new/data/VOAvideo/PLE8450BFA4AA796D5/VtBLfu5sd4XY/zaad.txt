Olympics Highlights China's Hoop Dreams
Uploaded on Aug 20, 2008
The U.S. basketball team's easy victory over the home team at the Olympics did not diminish Chinese enthusiasm for the sport. Basketball was introduced into China by Catholic missionaries in the early 1900s. But it was in the 1990s that the U.S. National Basketball Association, or NBA, began to cultivate China's potential market of 1.3 billion people. As VOA's Brian Padden reports, today the popularity of the NBA, particularly among young Chinese, rivals that of soccer [football] and even ping pong [table tennis]. And the Chinese idolize their home-grown NBA player, Yao Ming, who plays for the Houston Rockets.
