Zambians React to Having White Interim Leader After Sata's Death
Published on Nov 3, 2014
The body of Zambian president Michael Sata is back in his home country, after being flown in from London where he passed away last week. The country's interim president is a white man, born in Zambia when the country was still a British colony. Mariama Diallo reports on reactions to the interim government as the country prepares to bury its fifth president and awaits elections within 90 days.
