Modal verbs in simple, continuous, and perfect forms
Published on Nov 30, 2015
This lesson focuses on how we form simple, continuous, perfect and perfect continuous forms of modal verbs. https://www.skype-lessons.com/modal-v... The basic structure is the following: Modal + infinitive (could do) Modal + be + ____ing (could be doing) Modal + have + 3rd (could have done) Modal + have + been + ____ing (could have been doing) EXAMPLES: He must have been drinking last night because he got arrested. When he arrived, we should have been waiting for him. They might have been sleeping as they didn't hear me come in. More exercises and lessons on my website https://www.skype-lessons.com/
