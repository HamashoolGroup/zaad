INCORRECT "He's not going, but I'm." "I'm not wrong, you're." CORRECT "He's not going, but I am." "I'm not wrong, you are." "Rachel's a good friend of mine, but Richard isn't." "She went to school yesterday, but he didn't." "You can go if you want to. I ain't."
