Learn British English Free: Food Slang
Published on Mar 15, 2015
In this free British English lesson, I explain five slang terms using names of food. Please see below: Cheesy (adj.) e.g. cheesy chat-up line; cheesy music Go bananas = go crazy ?She went bananas when her husband told her how much the car cost.? Carrot (noun) = motivational tool ?To get your employees to put in more effort, you need to offer some form of carrot.? Milk something (phrasal verb) = to exaggerate something for extra praise or sympathy ?The football player milked his injury.? ?He did a good job but how long?s he going to milk that for?? Beef (noun) = problem ?What?s your beef?? ?What?s his beef??
