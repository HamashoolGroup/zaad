Years 2014 = twenty fourteen or "two-thousand and fourteen" 1999 = nineteen ninety-nine or ninety-nine 1981 = nineteen eighty-one or eighty one Ten years = a decade 100 years = a century 1,000 = a millennium 2000 -- 2010 = "the noughties" 1990 -- 1999 = "the nineties" 1980 -- 1989 = "the eighties" 1880 -- 1889 = "the eighteen-eighties" 2000 -- 2099 = "the twenty-first century" 1900 -- 1999 = "the twentieth century" Dates British English = day / month / year = DD/MM/(YY)YY 01/01/14 = 01/01/2014 = 1 Jan 2014 = 1 January 2014 = 1st of January 2014 15/3/09 = 15/03/2009 = 15 March 2009 = 15th of March 2009 Date of Birth The day / date you were born on. You must know how to say this for identity checks in particular. (Sometimes shortened to DOB). My date of birth is 14/11/1980 My DOB is 14/11/80 My date of birth is 14 November 1980 My DOB is the 14th of November, 1980 My birthday is 14/11 / 14 Nov / 14th of November Prepositions Use "in" to talk about actions which take place within a year / decade / century / millennium being discussed: "I was born in 1980." "I grew up in the eighties." "They got married in 2002. And they still are!" Use "on" to specify a particular date that an action took place on, or when something occurred: "I was born on the 14th of November, 1980." "He sadly passed away on the 7th of March, 2009." "I bought this car on 2 June 2011." "In the UK, Christmas is celebrated every year on December 25."
