?Chip in? and ?Cough up? ? British Phrasal Verbs for Money
Published on Feb 8, 2015
Chip in ? contribute to a collective payment: ?Kate?s leaving soon and we?re getting her a present, would you like to chip in?? Cough up ? pay for something that?s overdue or pay reluctantly: ?He?s owed me that cash for weeks, it?s about time he coughed up.? ?If you want a fancy car, you?re going to have to cough up.?
