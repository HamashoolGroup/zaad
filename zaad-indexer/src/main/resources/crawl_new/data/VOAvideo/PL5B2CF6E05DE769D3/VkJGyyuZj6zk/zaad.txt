US Parents Push for End to International Child Abduction
Uploaded on Apr 28, 2010
Parental child abduction is a growing international problem. It often involves a failed marriage, with one parent taking the children to a country where laws offer protection. One such destination is Japan, well-known for protecting Japanese parents - in most cases, mothers - who return home with their children. VOA's MacKenzie Babb has the story of how American parents are fighting to get their children back.
