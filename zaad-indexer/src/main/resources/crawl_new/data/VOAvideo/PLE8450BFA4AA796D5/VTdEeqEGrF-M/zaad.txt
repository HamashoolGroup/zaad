Olympic Torch Carried Through Beijing Streets for 3 Days
Uploaded on Aug 7, 2008
After a controversial journey that sparked protests in the U.S. and Europe, the Olympic torch made it to Beijing on August 6, 2008. In China's capital city, there was pageantry and celebration. But apart from the torch relay, there were also protests as four foreign activists were detained and expelled for objecting to Chinese government policies. And some U.S. athletes arriving for the games, which begin with opening ceremonies on August 8, 2008, wore masks they said would protect them from Beijing's polluted air. For VOA Producer Zulima Palacio, Mil Arcega narrates.
