Genome Initiative Urgently Moves to Freeze DNA Before Species Go Extinct
Published on Jul 28, 2015
Earth is in the midst of its sixth mass extinction. The last such event was caused by an asteroid 66 million years ago. It killed off the dinosaurs and practically everything else. So scientists are in a race against time to classify the estimated 11 million species alive today. So far only 2 million are described by science, and researchers are worried many will disappear before they even have a name. VOA?s Rosanne Skirble reports.
