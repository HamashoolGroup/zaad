Obama's Mosque Comments Fuel Controversy
Uploaded on Aug 18, 2010
U.S. President Barack Obama has ignited a political controversy with his comments about the rights of American Muslims to build an Islamic center and mosque near Ground Zero, the site of the 2001 terror attacks on New York City. The administration had claimed the issue was local, until the President made his remarks at the annual White House Iftar dinner celebrating Ramadan. Voice of America's Carolyn Presutti takes a look at the political fallout.
