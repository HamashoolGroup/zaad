40th anniversary of the Apollo Christmas Eve broadcast
Uploaded on Dec 29, 2008
Forty years ago, the crew of the Apollo 8 spacecraft became the first humans to orbit the moon. On that Christmas Eve in 1968, NASA astronauts Frank Borman, Jim Lovell and Bill Anders dedicated one of the mission's live transmissions to a reading from the Old Testament, in what has since come to be known as the Christmas Eve broadcast. VOA's Mil Arcega reports.
