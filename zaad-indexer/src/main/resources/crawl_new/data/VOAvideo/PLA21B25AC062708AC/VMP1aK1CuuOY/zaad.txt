Ground Zero Mosque Controversy Puts Many US Muslims on Defensive
Uploaded on Sep 1, 2010
The controversy over plans to build an Islamic center near the site of the 2001 terrorist attacks on New York City has put many Muslims living in United States on the defensive. But despite a rash of anti-Muslim rhetoric and possible hate crimes, VOA's Jerome Socolovsky reports that some Muslims see the mosque debate as an opportunity to reaffirm their place in American society.
