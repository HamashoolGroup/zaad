Africans in London Celebrate Papal Visit
Uploaded on Sep 14, 2010
It's nearly 30 years since Britain last received a papal visit. Since then the country's Catholic population has grown by around one million people. As Selah Hennessy reports, that's largely due to the influx of Catholic immigrants from around the world, especially Africa. From London, a look at how that's affected life at an inner city church and what its congregation makes of the Pope's visit.
