Swiss Museum Accepts Artworks From Nazi-Era Hoarder
Published on Nov 24, 2014
A museum in Switzerland has decided to accept hundreds of artworks from the son of a Nazi-era art dealer. German authorities seized the priceless collection of 1,280 artworks in 2012, hidden in an apartment in the city of Munich. Many of them were looted from Jewish families across Europe by the Nazis.
