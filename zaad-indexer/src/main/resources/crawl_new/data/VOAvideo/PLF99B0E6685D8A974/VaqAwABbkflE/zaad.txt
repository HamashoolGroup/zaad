Masai Demand Tanzanian Government Reject Plans for Hunting Reserve
Published on Dec 4, 2014
Around 5,000 Masai are gathering in their ancestral lands in Tanzania to discuss allegations that the government plans to evict them and sell off the land to build a game hunting reserve for rich Arabs. Tanzania?s president has dismissed the claims in a post on social media site Twitter. A global petition against the plans attracted over 2 million signatures. Henry Ridgwell reports from London.
