Volunteers Fill Aid Gap Left by Austrian Government
Published on Sep 24, 2015
In a small town outside Austria's capital, Vienna, a former school is housing thousands of refugees. The United Nations heavily criticized the camp for its intolerable and inhumane conditions. But while the government has been caught on the back foot, locals have stepped in to help. Denise Hruby and Rick Valenzuela report from Traiskirchen, Austria.
