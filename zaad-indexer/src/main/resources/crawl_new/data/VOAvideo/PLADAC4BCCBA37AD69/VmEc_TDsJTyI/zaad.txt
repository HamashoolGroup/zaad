Libyan Opposition Gives War Lessons to Youth
Uploaded on Apr 15, 2011
The uprising against the 41-year rule of Libyan leader Moammar Gadhafi began peacefully. But when government troops used force to suppress the demonstrations, they escalated into what increasingly looks like a civil war. Opposition forces, based mostly in the east, are fighting the much better-equipped and better-trained Gadhafi troops. But their leaders are trying to change that. VOA's Scott Bobb recently visited a military training camp near Benghazi and has this report.
