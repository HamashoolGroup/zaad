Complex gerunds and infinitives. Lesson 2
Published on Mar 20, 2016
In this class we focus on how gerunds and infinitives can be used in all 4 aspects (simple, continuous, perfect simple and perfect continuous). Please examine the following forms and examples: to do to be doing to have done (to have been done = passive) to have been doing Use perfect infinitive: 1) After adjectives 2) After certain verbs (claim, seem, hope, expect etc..) 3) After the passive structures (he is believed, they are known) 4) As subject Use perfect gerund 1) After prepositions 2) After certain verbs (admit, regret) 3) As subject Please go to my website for more classes on gerunds and infinitives, as well as many other topics http://skype-lessons.com/
