Section 9: Literacy Research Studies reveal how vital phonetic instruction is for improving literacy in our schools. Functional MRI studies show that systematic phonetic instruction rewires the brain! Struggling readers use higher level thinking skills to read, but with systematic teaching, their brains switch to using the language and auditory centers, like the brains of strong readers. The National Reading Panel has identified 5 strands of reading that are essential for building the foundations of literacy: Phonemic Awareness Systematic Phonics Vocabulary Development Fluency Reading Comprehension In this video, Denise discusses these and other literacy research findings and what they show us about teaching reading and spelling to all students.
