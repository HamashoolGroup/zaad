--- VIDEO TITLE: The subjunctive mood - English lessons with inlingua Vancouver --- VIDEO DESCRIPTION: The subjunctive mood - English lessons with inlingua Vancouver Wrong: I were born in 1987. Right: I was born in 1987. When would we use I + were? The subjunctive mood: If I were, if she were, if he were. What if: If I were a wizard. I would fly a broomstick. If she were to win the lottery, what do you think she'd do? If only: If I were taller, I would play basketball. If you were sixteen, you'd be able to drive. Reality: I think I was sixteen when my grandmother died, but if I was seventeen it would have been 2006. If it was snowing in the North East as much as forecasted. I bet the kids don't have school. Statement: School is cancelled. Suggestion: I politely suggest that school be cancelled forever. Statement: Dornell eats all ten bagels. Command: I command that he eat all ten bagels. Statement: Juan gives his homework attention. Urgency: It is essential that he give more attention. Statement: We are finished with subjunctives. Recommendation: I vote that we be finished with subjunctives. --- TEACHER: TASH Connect with us!
