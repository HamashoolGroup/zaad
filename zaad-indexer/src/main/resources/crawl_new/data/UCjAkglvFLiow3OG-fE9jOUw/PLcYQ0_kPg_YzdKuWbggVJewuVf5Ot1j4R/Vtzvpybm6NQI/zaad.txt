--- VIDEO TITLE: Tash - Similes (Like / As) - English Lessons with inlingua Vancouver --- VIDEO DESCRIPTION: Similes- A useful way to improve your creative writing, a simile is a figure of speech that is used to compare one thing to another. You can find similes everywhere, even in some pop songs. Similes can make our language more descriptive and enjoyable. Writers, poets, and songwriters make use of similes often to add depth and emphasize what they are trying to convey to the reader or listener. Similes can be funny, serious, mean, or creative. To help you identify a simile, know that the words ?like? or ?as? are typically used. Well-known similes are: ? ?as busy as a bee? comparing someone?s level of energy to a fast-flying bee ? "life is like a box of chocolates? comparing life to a box of chocolates- lots of choices and options. Connect with us!
