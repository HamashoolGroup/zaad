--- VIDEO TITLE: Pronouns Pt. 2 of 3 - Antecedents - English Lessons with inlingua Vancouver What is an antecedent? The noun that has been replaced. The pronoun usually refers to the closest preceding noun. "It was pitch black outside and my cat was missing. I grabbed my flash-light and listened for magic's purr." "There was a knock at the door". My mother had arrived. When do we use 'he' and when do we use 'him'? I like you better than him. The three pronoun cases Subjective case: the doer (subject) of the action. I throw the ball. Objective case: the receiver of the action. Throw the to me. Possessive casa: shows ownership. My throw won the game. Subjective I sing You sing He/She sings It sings We sing They sing Who sings? Objective Sing to me Joe sings to you Sing to her/him Sing to it Sing to us Sing to them To whom does Joe sing? Possessive My song Your song Her/His song its song Our song Their song Whose song? Tricks for deciding pronoun cases: add or delete words Mom cooked dinner for Brian and I - Mom cooked dinner for Brian and (for) I. Mom cooked dinner for Brian and (for) me. Her and I went to the Mall. Her (went) and I went to the Mall. She (went) and I went to the Mall. Us eighth graders are studying algebra. She is taller than me - She is taller than me (am tall) She is taller than I (am tall). Turn the sentences around: The best students are Tyan and I. I am the best student. The winner is him.
