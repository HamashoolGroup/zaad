A vet works with many animals-dogs, cats, cows, etc. Parenthetical expressions: I reminded Mum, in case she'd forgotten, that it's my anniversary. Theu usually cost, if you get a good deal, around $200. After greetings / before closing: Dear Miranda, I love your pizza. Sincerely, Tash. Two parts of sentence in contrast: I ordered pizza, not lasagna. I'm going out with Mish, not Lucy. Titles AFTER names: Arnold Samson, Ph.D., is my English teacher. Dr. Samson, is my English teacher. Before and after appositives: Our principal, John Bossman, gave a great speech. (something explaining a noun) Principal Bossman gave a great speech. John Bossman, our principal, gave a great speech.
