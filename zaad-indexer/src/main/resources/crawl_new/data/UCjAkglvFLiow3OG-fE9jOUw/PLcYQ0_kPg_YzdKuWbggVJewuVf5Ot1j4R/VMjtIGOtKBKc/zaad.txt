--- VIDEO TITLE: Tricky Words - Lie vs Lay - English Lessons with inlingua Vancouver --- VIDEO DESCRIPTION: Tricky Words - Lie vs Lay - English Lessons with inlingua Vancouver Lie is a still verb. People lie on beds. Dogs lie on people. Lay is an active verb. A person picks up a dog and lays it on a blanket. I will lay the baby in the crib. Lie (to lie down on a bed) Today I lie in bed. Yesterday I lay in bed. Many times I have lain in bed. Yesterday I was lying in bed all day. Lying in bed all day is boring. Lay (to place something, put something down) Today I lay the the book on the counter. Yesterday I laid the book on the counter. Many times I have laid the book on the counter. Yesterday I was laying the book on the counter when my mum came home. Laying books on the counter is untidy. --- TEACHER: TASH Connect with us!
