Chock-a-Block | British English Expression
Published on Jun 14, 2015
? The British English Expression - Chock-a-block Chock-a-block means very full, packed, or congested. Sometimes the bridge is so chock-a-block with pedestrians that it's difficult to get to work. It was impossible to find anywhere to park as the car park was chock-a-block with cars.
